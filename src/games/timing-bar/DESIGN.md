# Papel y Tinta

The art direction for "Al Centro". Every visual decision in `Renderer.ts` and `style.css` answers to it. The game is a drafting instrument, not an arcade cabinet: it lives in the same cream-and-ink world as the landing page and the room overlays, and it should feel like a precision ruler you are reading, not a neon toy you are pounding.

The material is paper and ink. The field is warm cream (`#efeee6`) on a slightly deeper cream backdrop (`#d6d3bd`); the marks are near-black ink (`#111`). This is the palette of a good notebook, a technical drawing, a measuring tool machined and printed rather than lit. Nothing glows. Depth comes from flat borders (thick, 2.5px, ink) and a single hard drop shadow, the way a card sits on a desk — never from gradients pretending to be light.

The ruler is the protagonist and it must read as an instrument. Ticks are evenly indexed hairlines — minor every 2%, major every 10% — drawn faint (ink at 14% alpha) so the marks recede and the marker dominates. The baseline is a confident solid ink stroke. Precision is legible rhythm: the eye trusts exact spacing and distrusts wobble, so the tick grid is mathematically regular and the center is unmistakable — a dashed target line in the deep accent, framed by the one splash of color the composition allows.

Color is scarce and it means something. Cyan (`#00f0ff` / deep `#0091a6`) is the target and the system accent, inherited from the landing so this game belongs to the set; it marks the center band and the "you did well" states. Warm coral-red (`#ff5a3c`) is reserved for the perfect hit alone — the reward, spent almost never, so that when the ring flashes at dead center it lands. Muted olive (`#6f6d5e`) carries everything secondary: labels, poor stops, the quiet metadata of the top bar. A stop is scored in color: coral for perfect, deep cyan for good, olive for a miss — the player learns the palette as a scale.

Motion is tense, not busy. One thing moves — the marker — and it moves cleanly, edge to edge, accelerating round over round; its stillness at the moment of the stop is the whole drama. Feedback is a single crisp gesture: a `+points` number floats where the marker froze, a pill banner pops once and holds, a ring expands only on perfection. Type is heavy and confident (weights 800-900), uppercase for structure, tabular figures so numbers never jitter. Restraint everywhere in the field is what lets the single moving mark, and the single center hit, carry all the meaning.
